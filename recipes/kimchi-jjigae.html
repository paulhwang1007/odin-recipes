Kimchi Jjigae

Description

Kimchi jjigae is a hearty and comforting Korean stew that highlights the bold flavors of kimchi, a staple fermented vegetable dish in Korean cuisine. Typically prepared with aged kimchi, which has a stronger, tangier flavor, the stew is simmered with pork, tofu, onions, and often other vegetables like zucchini or mushrooms. The base of kimchi jjigae is a rich broth made from anchovy stock or sometimes pork or beef broth, infused with the pungent and spicy notes of kimchi.

Seasonings such as gochugaru and gochujang add depth and a mild heat to the stew, creating a robust and savory dish that warms both the body and soul. Kimchi jjigae is typically served bubbling hot in a communal pot, accompanied by a bowl of steamed rice, and enjoyed with various banchan, embodying the essence of Korean comfort food.

Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Garlic
  • Kimchi
  • White onion
  • Green onion
  • Gochujang
  • Gochugaru
  • Kimchi Juice
  • Anchovy Broth
  • Soy Sauce
  • SUgar
  • Tofu
  • Spam
  • Enoki Mushroom

Steps

  1. Season meat with salt and fry
  2. Add minced garlic
  3. Add cut up kimchi
  4. Fry for a bit
  5. Add gochujang, gochugaru, sliced onions, diced green onions
  6. Add your stock and kimchi juice
  7. Add soy sauce and sugar
  8. Simmer
  9. Add tofu, spam, and mushroom
  10. Enjoy!
